Obesity is not about being 'Fat'

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), Obesity is a chronic complex disease where one's health is impaired by excessive fat deposits. Obesity is the opposite extremity of malnutrition, possibly caused by an imbalanced diet and/or lack of physical activity. Obesity may also be the product of obesogenic environments, psycho-social factors and genetic variants.

Obesity is the onset of multiple diseases and health complications. The risks of Noncommunicable Diseases (NCDs), such as Type 2 diabetes, cardiovascular disease and certain types of cancer can be exacerbated by obesity. Bone health and reproduction are also negatively affected by obesity.

For adults, WHO defines properly diagnosed obesity cases as those where a person's Body Mass Index (BMI) is greater than or equal to 30.
