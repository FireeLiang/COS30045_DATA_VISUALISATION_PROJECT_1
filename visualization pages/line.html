Analysis of Obesity Rates in Europe

  1. Eastern Europe (Red Line): This region shows the highest increase in obesity rates, rising sharply from around 20% in 2014 to nearly 30% in 2022. The trend indicates a significant rise in obesity levels, making Eastern Europe the most affected region among those analyzed.
  2. Central Europe (Orange Line): Central Europe follows a similar upward trajectory, reaching about 27% by 2022. The rising trend suggests a growing prevalence of obesity in this region, though slightly lower compared to Eastern Europe.
  3. Southern Europe (Purple Line): Southern Europe shows a gradual increase in obesity rates, starting from around 19% in 2014 and rising to over 22% by 2022. The pace of growth is slower compared to Eastern and Central Europe.
  4. Northern Europe (Green Line): Northern Europe starts at approximately 16% in 2014 and increases gradually to about 18% in 2022. The trend shows moderate growth in obesity rates compared to other regions.
  5. Western Europe (Blue Line): Western Europe maintains the lowest rate of obesity among the analyzed regions. Starting from around 16% in 2014, it shows a slight increase to around 18% by 2022. The rate of growth is steady but remains relatively lower compared to the other regions.
